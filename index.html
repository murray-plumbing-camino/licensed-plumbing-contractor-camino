Key Terminology

System mapping
A method of identifying how fixtures, shutoffs, and branches connect so symptoms can be traced to a specific zone.
Isolation testing
Targeted checks used to separate supply-side issues from fixture problems or drainage restrictions.
Material transition
A connection point where piping types change, often requiring compatible joining methods to prevent premature failures.
Drain pathway evaluation
An assessment of how wastewater moves through branches and main lines to identify restrictions, alignment concerns, or intrusion risks.
Inspection readiness
Work quality and documentation that support review when a permit or inspection is applicable to the scope.

Process & Diagnostics

The technical process typically begins with symptom confirmation and a short orientation of supply and drain routes. Diagnostics then focus on isolating the defect and identifying contributors such as environmental exposure, material fatigue, or drainage restrictions. Once the failure point is validated, the corrective plan prioritizes compatibility and access constraints. After completion, verification confirms stable operation and supports readiness for local review when required.
